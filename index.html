UNLOCK THE POWER OF LOCATION INTELLIGENCE

Who We Are

Polardot is a Geospatial and ICT consulting company based in Nairobi, Kenya. We specialize in using space technologies for location data analytics, location intelligence, remote sensing, photogrammetry, mapping, and cloud-based geospatial software development.

Our goal is to create cost-effective, easy to use, and sustainable Geo-IT solutions which have a positive impact across both communities and businesses in Africa.

Our Work

What we do

Data Collection

We integrate location data from a variety of sources such as autonomous drones, earth observation satellite imagery, internet of things(IoT) sensors, global positioning systems(GPS), airborne sensors, databases, web, and social media data mining, and open-source datasets.

Generate Insights

Maps not only give data context but also provide an opportunity to present an engaging and valuable business case. Our expertise in spatial analysis will help you form a compelling narrative, both relevant to your business and drawn from actionable insight..

Our Natural Language

Our team has extensive academic and practical knowledge in the principles of measurement, spatial analysis, coordinate transformations, and map projections. We value the fundamentals of geomatics and spatial science. Maps are our natural language.

Drive Competitive Advantage

Develop insights, create value for your customers and make internal processes faster. By combining best-practice software engineering and spatial analysis, we’ll make your data work for you.

Products

Some of the products we create

These are web applications hosted in the cloud to store, analyze, visualize and interact with spatial data. This data is integrated from various sources depending on the client’s scope of work.

This is a graphical indicator that is used in agricultural maps to identify plants that are under stress, differentiate bare soil from grass or forest, and also analyze various crop stages. This data comes from either satellite or drone-based sensors that detect and quantify the presence of live green vegetation using the reflected light in the visible and near-infrared bands of the light spectrum.

These are maps that indicate the physical delineation of property boundaries and determination of dimensions, areas, and certain rights associated with properties, whether they are on land, water, or defined by natural or artificial features.

This is a three-dimensional visualization of the surface of the earth which is made up of thousands or even millions of georeferenced points from drone images. Each point contains several measurements, including its coordinates along the X, Y, and Z-axes. From the point clouds, it is possible to extract surfaces, objects, 3D models, plans, and sections. Point clouds are data-rich, and you can use them to analyze elevations, distances, and volumes.

These maps are used to display the height, depth, size, and location of any man-made or natural features on a given parcel of land, as well as the changes or contours in elevation throughout the parcel. Together with a boundary survey, topographic surveys are used by architects and engineers to create accurate and appropriate designs based on existing conditions. We use drones and global positioning systems to accurately produce maps and contours of a given site.

This is a geometrically correct aerial image that is composed of many still drone images that are stitched together to provide a view similar to the satellite view in Google Maps. However, the data is far more accurate and up to date. The ability to zoom in and out on key points of interest, identify potential issues, and track progress, all without distorting the image is a powerful tool across many industries, including real estate, construction, property management, and property inspections.

This is a digital representation of the bare earth’s surface obtained from geo-referenced drone images. It has information about the earth’s terrain consisting of natural features such as drainage lines, faults, ridgelines, troughs, coast/shorelines, peaks, and pits. This terrain information can be used in various geo-related applications such as volume and profile calculations of earthworks, hydrology, natural hazard process modeling, and geomorphological mapping.

This is a digital representation of the earth’s surface obtained from geo-referenced drone images it includes both the natural and built/artificial features of the environment. It includes elevations of vegetation and buildings. This data can be useful for landscape modeling, city modeling, and visualization applications.
